Understanding Quote Types

The New Quote form offers two shipment profiles—Hotshot and Air. Each option optimizes pricing, transit time assumptions, and accessorial availability for a different style of expedited freight. Use this guide to choose the best fit before you begin a quote.

Hotshot (Ground Expedited)

Hotshot moves leverage dedicated sprinter vans or straight trucks for rapid point-to-point delivery without terminal handoffs.

Ideal Use Cases

  • Urgent freight within the continental US or cross-border to Canada.
  • Shipments up to roughly 10,000 lbs or 12 linear feet that fit on a single vehicle.
  • Pickup or delivery locations that require flexible scheduling or dock-high access.

Pros

  • Door-to-door service with the same driver, minimizing handling risk.
  • Competitive pricing for regional or multi-state runs.
  • Accessory options like liftgate or residential service where available.

Considerations

  • Transit times depend on road conditions and driver hours-of-service limits.
  • Limited capacity for oversized freight compared to full truckload equipment.
  • Weather and traffic can introduce delays during long-haul runs.

Air (Next-Flight-Out)

Air quotes combine courier pickup, commercial air cargo, and final-mile delivery to achieve the fastest possible transit time.

Ideal Use Cases

  • Critical shipments that must arrive the same day or overnight.
  • High-value or time-sensitive components headed to maintenance or production lines.
  • Long-distance moves where ground transit would miss customer deadlines.

Pros

  • Fastest delivery option with predictable flight schedules.
  • Dedicated monitoring from pickup through final mile.
  • Ability to route internationally with customs coordination.

Considerations

  • Higher base cost compared to ground alternatives.
  • Dimensional weight minimums can apply to bulky but lightweight freight.
  • Hazmat and lithium battery restrictions may limit eligibility.

Choosing the Right Option

Start with the customer's deadline and shipment profile:

  • Select Hotshot when the freight fits on a dedicated ground unit and delivery within one to two days is acceptable.
  • Choose Air if missing a same-day or next-day deadline would shut down production, incur penalties, or harm safety.
  • When unsure, compare both quotes and consult operations for capacity or compliance guidance.

After selecting the quote type, complete the remaining form details and review any automatically enabled accessorials before sending the customer-facing proposal.
